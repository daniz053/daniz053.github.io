CROSS PLATFORM STORYTELLING

In addition to answering these questions, we were asked to code a website which reflects our answers. The creative direction was left open-ended with only a select few requests from our professor. STORIES AS NETWORKS

This website was inspired by an exceprt from Junichiro Tanizaki's In Praise of Shadows. I was inspired by his vivid imagery of traditional Japanese architecture, as well as the arbitrary relationship between shadow and light. Requested to not use imagery, this project pushed my boundaries and made me step outside of my comfort zone.

PARTNER DOCUMENTATION

This assignment was to practice documenting other's works in an exciting way. My partner was Shravani. I was asked to observe my partner pairing and review their Stories as Networks project. Speaking with them about any highlights and main design details that are important to communicate, I created a webpage that presented their project as though it were a case study. I included the name of the project, a link to view it, and at least one of the documentation techniques taught in class.

ELASTIC COLLECTION

This project was completed through Parson’s Core Interaction 2 creative coding class. We were asked to collect, or create, a collection of 50 to 100 items. Our collections needed to include at least five different data type affiliated with it. Additionally, we required to have a visual consistency between the items collected. From there, we were asked to trade our collections with an assigned classmate. Through this, we designed a website for our partner’s collection connected to the API. Through this experience, our goal was to make use of an API to design, and build an engaging and cohesive data visual.
